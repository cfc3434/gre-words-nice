bluster [ˈblʌstər] 【考法1】 v．狂妄自大地大声说：to speak in a loudly arrogant or bullying manner 例 He was blustering alone in the meeting, which triggered wide dissatisfaction．他一人在会议上夸夸其谈，引得众人不满。 近 roar, clamor, rattle 反 whisper　小声嘀咕 【考法2】 n．喧闹的状态：a state of noisy, confused activity 例 a mayor who got things done without a lot of bluster　一个能把事情低调解决的市长 近 disturbance, pandemonium, tumult, turmoil 派 blustering adj．大吵大闹的
