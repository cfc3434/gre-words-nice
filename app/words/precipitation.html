precipitation [prɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃn] 【考法1】 n．沉积物，尤指降水：something precipitated as a deposit on the earth of hail, mist, rain, sleet, or snow 例 The storm brought several inches of precipitation．风暴带来的降水量达数英寸。 近 deposit, sediment 【考法2】 n．仓促：excited and often showy or disorderly speed 例 I fear that I may have acted with some precipitation on this matter, so I would like to reconsider．我害怕自己在这个事情上考虑得太仓促了，因此我想再想想。 近 haste, hastiness, hustle, precipitousness, rush 反 deliberation　深思熟虑
