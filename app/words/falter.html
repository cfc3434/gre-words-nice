falter [ˈfɔːltər] 【考法1】 vi．蹒跚：to walk unsteadily 近 lurch, stagger, stumble, teeter, totter, wobble 【考法2】 vi．犹豫，踌躇：to be unsteady in purpose or action, as from loss of courage or confidence 例 Mr. Garrison never once faltered in his demand that slavery be unconditionally abolished．盖瑞森先生从不对他废除奴隶的要求存有任何的犹豫。 近 balance, halt, hesitate, vacillate, waver 派 faltering adj．犹豫的
